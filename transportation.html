Vision Zero - Houston, TX

Team Project with Emmy Park and June Jaegal

Role: Front End Developer

Skills: R, ArcGIS Map SDK for JavaScript, Html, CSS, Figma

In order to make these necessary safety interventions, planners for the City of Houston need all kinds of information - how many crashes are happening on this road, the characteristics of the road, what else is happening in this road area, etc. The goal of this project was to consolidate information about street and intersection features into a dashboard, so that planners can have all the information they need in one location. We developed a safety index so that planners can quickly see where safety interventions should be prioritized given present and potential future conditions in the city. This will allow the City of Houston Public Works Department to efficiently and proactively implement safety interventions, improving traffic safety and progressing Vision Zero across the city.

With the prediction of the number of crashes using machine learning models, we developed a safety index based on this number and then finally visualized that, along with other road data, on the dashboard. The dashboard was implemented in html/css/Javascript with ArcGIS Map SDK for JS and Calcite Design system. With the basic functions, such as filtering by data types and time, the dashboard also includes Google Street View, graphs, and extracting data through geographical relationships, which is a strong information system for city planners.

Packer Park Pedestrian & Bicycle Improvement Plan

Team Project with Jingyi Li, Ziyi Tang, Timothy Oliver and Regy Septian

Role: Planner; Strategies Editor

Skills: ArcGIS Online, Streetmix

Located in South Philadelphia, PA, Packer Park neighborhood is a center for recreation and entertainment. The FDR Park and several sports fields attract thousands of citizens every week. However, accessing Packer Park is still dominant by private vehicles. Although there is a subway station and several bus routes crossing the neighborhood, the high injury networks make the environment unsafe. Therefore, we analyzed the current transportation situation in Packer Park and built an improvement plan to strengthen the basic infrastructure and promote multi-modal transportation. This document gives more detail to the residential and recreational focused Packer Park Neighborhood and reasoning behind recommendations alongside influential initiatives it aims to complement.

Boston Bike Share Analysis in Boston, MA

Personal Project

Skills: R

The project addresses the re-balancing challenge in Boston's bike-share program by predicting bike pickup availability. Utilizing data from Blue Bikes and weather sources, the project incorporates temporal features and weather variables to enhance prediction accuracy. Key findings show higher bike demand in central areas, particularly near universities, and varying demand based on time of day and weather conditions. The most effective model is a space-time-lag regression model, combining spatial, temporal, and time lag features, offering valuable insights for improving bike distribution and managing demand. Skills used include data collection, time series analysis, geospatial analysis, and statistical modeling.

Public Land Access for Populations of Wisconsin

Team Project with Kayla Olness, Eli Samuelson and Joseph Rosnow

Role: GIS Analyst; Co-author

Skills: ArcMap

Some urban areas are closer to public land than others. Historically, a community's access to public land has relied heavily on its geographic location. We want to evaluate which populations in Wisconsin have the easiest access to public land. By observing the demographics of each municipality, we hope to understand which communities and which variables have a correlation with distance to public lands. Because access to nature is important for all people, we will be assessing the ease at which people who live in metropolitan areas can get to public land, in order to highlight differences in accessibility between populations. We used network analysis and closest facility analysis in ArcGIS Pro, and the result shows that in northern Wisconsin, access to public land parcels will require a longer drive due to a reduced road network. Many municipalities in northern Wisconsin also have lower average annual household income than municipalities in southern Wisconsin. Southern Wisconsin almost exhibits the opposite trends found in northern Wisconsin as a result of higher population density and a better road network.
