Primary Medical Centers Allocation, Southeast Guizhou, China

Team Project with Jingyi Li

Role: Data Analyst; Co-author

The distribution of medical facilities in China shows a significant gap between the eastern and western regions. When examining the concentration of healthcare resources, the mean values in Western Regions are much lower compared to other areas. The influence of economic growth on the concentration of healthcare resources is both significant and positive. The GDP per capita in Guizhou is below $50,000 and is lesser than the national average. Educational resources, specifically the number of colleges and prospective students prepared to work in hospitals, are also lacking

Using capacitated P-median model, the project addresses the question where to locate 120 county-level Grade A primary medical centers in Qiandongnan Prefecture, Guizhou, China and how to assign demand in each residential area to those medical centers to minimize the distance cost between them.

Story map: People of the Sturgeon

Work Project: supervised by David Hart and Anne Moser

Role: Story map editor; GIS analyst

This project cooperated with University of Wisconsin Sea Grant Institute. Originated from the book People of the Sturgeon: Wisconsin's love affair with an ancient fish, the story map includes multiple interactive maps to enhance the visualization of the geographical aspect of sturgeon's habitat. The multi-media also helps readers to understand the content better. The story map has 6 sections, which fully demonstrate the human-sturgeon relationship and the effort of protection.
