Primary Medical Centers Allocation, Southeast Guizhou, China

Team Project with Jingyi Li

Role: Data Analyst; Co-author

Skills: ArcGIS Pro, CPlex

The distribution of medical facilities in China shows a significant gap between the eastern and western regions. When examining the concentration of healthcare resources, the mean values in Western Regions are much lower compared to other areas. The influence of economic growth on the concentration of healthcare resources is both significant and positive. The GDP per capita in Guizhou is below $50,000 and is lesser than the national average. Educational resources, specifically the number of colleges and prospective students prepared to work in hospitals, are also lacking

Using capacitated P-median model, the project addresses the question where to locate 120 county-level Grade A primary medical centers in Qiandongnan Prefecture, Guizhou, China and how to assign demand in each residential area to those medical centers to minimize the distance cost between them.

Drug Overdose Risk Prediction in Mesa, AZ

Team Project with Jingyi Li

Role: Data Analyst

Skills: R

In this project, we are going to predict drug overdose incidents to help the city's government, such as the law enforcement agencies and the public health department, better allocate prevention and medical resources. The location of our case study is the City of Mesa in Arizona, which has a high fatal overdose rate for many years. Our predictive model is an application to the government staff, which enables an efficient and accurate use of limited public health resources, and it is for education use in communities which have a high risk of overdose incidents to teach residents how to prevent and report when they see an overdose incident. The application provides clear map visualizations of the incidents' location and incident density, but considering about the privacy of patients, we do not publish the name and the birth date of the patient.

Impact of Covid-19 on Full-service Ethnic Restaurants in Madison, WI

Undergraduate Capstone Project with Junxian Xu

Role: Team Leader; Data Collector; Co-author

Skills: ArcGIS Online, Qualtrics

The Covid-19 pandemic is a challenge for small businesses, especially for non-chain restaurants, because of the restrictions. To understand how the Covid-19 pandemic affected the geography pattern of full-service ethnic restaurants and how the effects are related to customer preferences changes, we used Madison, Wisconsin, as a case study. Through mapping the distribution of verified records of full-service ethnic restaurants in 2019 and the affected restaurants, divided by permanently closed, temporarily closed, and relocated, from 2020 to 2022, American restaurants and Asian restaurants near the capitol and campus area, are affected the most. The data from a public survey of Madison residents and interviews of Asian restaurant owners revealed that fear of infection and forcible policies directly related to dining in fewer times than before. Although online order platforms and government funding are usable strategies, they could be more efficient and impeccable. The overall negative effect of Covid-19 has not recovered in 2022.

NYC Anti-Asian Crime Map (2010-2021)

Team Project with Gengkui Liu

Role: Team Leader; Javascript Programmer

Skills: Html/CSS/Javascript - leaflet, D3

New York is one of the most serious cities with a high Anti-Asian hate crime rate. According to Stop AAPI Hate, a national non-profit coalition, there are 9081 incidents reported between March and June 2020, doubling the number 4548 last year. Therefore, it is essential for the public and the government to pay more attention to the racial hate in our society. Asian people study here, work here, and they should have equal rights to live safely without being attacked unreasonably. The purpose of the interactive map is to compare the anti-Asian crime data acorss 77 police districts in New York with a coordinated line chart to compare the data of each police districts from 2010 to 2021 (Season 1 to Season 4). The crime news dot map is to show some serious crimes that are reported in News Article from the website during the pandemic.

Welcome Home: California Covid-19 Homeless Impact (2020-2021)

Personal Project

Skills: ArcGIS Pro, Adobe Illustrator

COVID-19 pandemic has broken out in California since March, 2020. The increased cases reached the first highest point in July. On July 23, there are 10647 new cases in California. In December, the increased cases reached the second highest point. With the restrictions of wearing masks and stay home order, daily new cases decreased in 2021. On April 6, the total new cases are only 2261. Homeless is one of the social issues generated during the pandemic. Homeless people are concentrated in Bay area, including San Francisco and Los Angeles county. Project Roomkey, supported by local government, is the main project to provided shelter rooms.
