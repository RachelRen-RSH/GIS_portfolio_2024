Story map: Allocating Resources to Minimize Human-Coyote Problems, Long Beach, CA

Personal Project

Skills: ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, ArcGIS Storymap, ArcGIS Dashboard

Native to Mexico's grasslands and North America's deserts, coyotes have spread across the continent. Differing from other large North American carnivores, many coyotes reside in areas with human developments due to rapid urbanization.

The storymap uses ArcGIS Suitability Modeler to analyze the areas that have a high priority in reduce coyotes' attacking risks. The analysis considers five factors: Children percentage by census tracts, proximity to water systems, residential areas, parks and recreational centers, and schools. The data is in the raster format and is geo-processed through kernel density, reclassification and distance accumulation tools.

Story map: People of the Sturgeon

Work Project: supervised by David Hart and Anne Moser

Role: Story map editor; GIS analyst

Skills: ArcGIS Pro, ArcGIS Online, ArcGIS Storymap, ArcGIS 3D Scene

This project cooperated with University of Wisconsin Sea Grant Institute. Originated from the book People of the Sturgeon: Wisconsin's love affair with an ancient fish, the story map includes multiple interactive maps to enhance the visualization of the geographical aspect of sturgeon's habitat. The multi-media also helps readers to understand the content better. The story map has 6 sections, which fully demonstrate the human-sturgeon relationship and the effort of protection.
